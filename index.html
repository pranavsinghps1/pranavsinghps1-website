Pranav Singh

I'm a second year Masters in Computer Science candidate at NYU. I am interested in deep learning, self- supervised learning, computer vision, resource-efficient learning, and their application to solve problems at scale. Most recently, my research work has been focused on the application of AI in healthcare.

Currently, I am working with Prof. Jacopo Cirrone, PhD and Prof. Elena Sizikova on developing classification and segmentation pipelines for Auto-immune diseases, a field vastly underrepresented in medical imaging and correlated to Covid-19.

Before this, from 2019 until 2021, I was involved in developing object detection, low-light imaging, reverse kinematics and reinforcement learning-based robotic arm control and simultaneous localisation and Mapping (SLAM) algorithms for autonomous rovers. These rovers were developed for Mars Society's URC 2020 (University Rover Challange), where we finished as International semi-finalists [System Acceptance Review]. I lead the AI and CS subsystem team for Mars Society South Asia's (MSSA) IRDC 2020 (International Rover Design Challenge, formerly Indian Rover Design Challenge), where we finished in the top 30 worldwide.
